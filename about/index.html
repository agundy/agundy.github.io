Growing up I was a natural explorer and inventor, playing with technology, tearing old appliances apart, reading everything I could get my hands on and creating new products was normal entertainment for me. Many 12 year olds dream of being sports stars, firefighters, rock stars, etc, I only wanted to be an engineer. Through high school I took many classes to realize this dream and then I went to Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute where I realized my dreams. Work

I recently graduated from RPI with a Bachelors in Computer Science. I now work full-time as a software engineer at BookBub, in Boston, MA. I also an Inventor at The Inventor's Guild, as an Inventor I work as a software developer with an award winning team of entrepreneurial and technical students and professionals on consulting projects, competitions and more.

I am passionate about open source software and I worked with RCOS(Rensselaer Center for Open Source Software) as a Coordinator and software developer while at RPI. Now that I graduated I mentor and provide expertise for current students. I worked on building open source culture, encouraging developer growth and organizing events and meetings. I previously worked with Rensselaer's Dean of Students Office's Summer STEM camps with middle and high school students introducing them to college life and inspiring and preparing them for careers in science, technology, engineering and math.

I previously worked at Onshape as co-op on the Operations team working on DevOps. I worked at Onshape starting in late May 2015 and going through December 2016. While there I worked on deployment and build automation, monitoring and alerting, and other miscellaneous projects needed by the team. Some of the various technologies I used at Onshape including Ansible, Puppet, Python, MongoDB, Java, Angular, Logstash, and Elasticsearch. While working at Onshape I also did some parttime work for Sia building a file sharing platform using Sia's decentralized storage backend.
